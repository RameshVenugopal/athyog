What Is Chair Yoga?
Chair Yoga is a modified form of traditional yoga in which postures (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and mindful movements are performed while sitting on a chair or using a chair for support. It is especially beneficial for individuals who sit for prolonged hours, have limited mobility, experience work-related stress, or cannot comfortably perform floor-based yoga. Chair Yoga combines gentle movement, controlled breathing, and neurological relaxation.
Why Chair Yoga Is Medically Relevant Today
Prolonged sitting is associated with musculoskeletal disorders, poor circulation, metabolic slowdown, increased risk of diabetes, hypertension, obesity, stress, and burnout. Chair Yoga activates muscles, improves circulation, and calms the nervous system even while seated.
Scientific & Medical Benefits
1. Musculoskeletal Health: Improves spinal mobility, reduces stiffness, activates core muscles, and supports posture correction.  2. Circulatory & Metabolic Benefits: Enhances blood flow, oxygen delivery, glucose regulation, and supports weight management.  3. Nervous System Regulation: Stimulates parasympathetic response, reduces stress hormones, improves emotional stability and cardiovascular balance.
